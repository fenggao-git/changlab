Anodization of Aluminum

 



 
 

  We are investigating ways of fabricating highly regimented nanoporous material by alumina anodization. The mechanism behind the  hexagonal pore formation has been deciphered and the pore dimensions  can now be correlated directly to the applied voltage and to the  electrolyte composition. Pores with diameters ranging from 20 to 200  nm and with depths upto microns can be fabricated. We are currently  improving the regularity of the pores. This research is in collaboration with Professor A.E. Miller's group.
